SCHOOL OUTREACH

Past Examples

“STEM pathways for foster students” The EWB foundation worked along with the pyjama foundation in order to bring EWB volunteers from Queensland university of Technology to tech STEM to a group of foster children. This an important project as there are currently over 50,000 children living in foster care in Australia who are facing many hardships due to their circumstances that effect their study and overall academic performance this can be seen in the NAPLAN results which show that these children are the lowest performing group.

Requiremnts

This program is all about teaching and transferring our skills in engineering and technical professions to students in communities who need our help , so to work in the school outreach program you will need to have a background or be currently working or studying in the engineering and technical professions.

FAQ

Q:Do I have to be an engineer worker or student to participate?
A: Yes , but if you feel like you have experience in a different area we would like to know how you think your skills will be able to help us and will possibly be put on the team.
